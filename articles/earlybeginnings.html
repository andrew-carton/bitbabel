BitBabel - A Journey Through the Bits and Bytes

Description: Early beginnings of a blog

Date:2024-02-23


Hello, world! I’m embarking on an exciting journey into the heart of embedded systems, and I’d like to invite you along. This is BitBabel, a blog where I’ll document my exploration of the intricate world of bits and bytes.

My journey will take me through the realms of embedded C, assembly language, digital logic, and computer architecture. I’ll be using small devices as my playground, delving deep into their inner workings to understand the magic that brings them to life.

The name BitBabel didn’t come to me right away. Initially, I thought of “BitBabbel”, with ‘Bit’ representing the domain I was exploring, and ‘Babbel’ symbolizing the act of writing a blog about it. But then, the idea of “BitBabel” struck me.

Babel, a homophone of Babbel, took me back to the biblical story of the Tower of Babel, where everyone once spoke the same language. I began to see parallels between the Tower and the world of technology. The low-level bits and bytes are the building blocks of our technological tower, and just as the Tower of Babel represents how far we’ve come, so too does the evolution of computer science.

We’ve reached a point where technology is advancing at an unprecedented rate, building upon layers and layers of abstractions. We’ve developed natural language processing that can translate languages in real-time, and AI that can reason about technology in natural language. It’s as if we’ve built our own Tower of Babel.

But just as the biblical tower was struck down, I see a need to return to the basics. To embrace minimalism, to resist the urge to build further upon the same abstractions. It’s time to appreciate the diversity in design, to rediscover the essence of computer science that lies in understanding how everything works.

There’s a certain thrill in starting from scratch, in learning the humble beginnings of technology, and in embracing how everything is built. So, join me as I embark on this journey, as I unravel the mysteries of the bits and bytes, and as I build my own Tower of Babel, one bit at a time.
